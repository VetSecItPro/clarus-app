## Core Philosophy

**Users want the 80/20 in plain language, not a research report.**

1. **Tell them immediately if it's worth their time** (Quality score upfront)
2. **Summarize what the VIDEO says** (Not state facts - describe the content)
3. **Flag what's sketchy inline** (Only cautions/fact-checks, no confirmations)
4. **Give them a clear verdict** (Should I watch/read?)

**Key balance:** Compress the meta-analysis, EXPAND the content summary for understanding.

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY GUIDELINES

### Style: "The Video Says" Not "Facts Are"

**Wrong:**
> Graphene is 200x stronger than steel.

**Right:**
> The video explains that graphene is 200x stronger than steel.

**Wrong:**
> Skeleton Technologies has super-batteries on market.

**Right:**  
> Showcases Skeleton Technologies' super-batteries and reports their claim of 1 million charge cycles.

Always frame it as describing what the VIDEO covers/says/explains/reports.

### Use Bullets and Scannable Format

**Make it skimmable:**
- Break into clear sections with headers
- Use bullets extensively
- Bold key numbers and names
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)

**Example structure:**
```markdown
### Real Products Shipping

**Company Name - Product:**
- The video covers how...
- Lists applications: X, Y, Z
- Reports claim of [number]

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **AI Note:** [Only if there's a caution]

**Next Company:**
- Pattern continues...
```

### AI Commentary: Only Warnings, Not Confirmations

**DON'T include:**
- ‚úì "This is well-documented" (removes confirmatory notes)
- ‚úì "The company exists" (obvious)
- ‚úì "This is verified science" (unless reader would doubt it)

**DO include:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è "Manufacturer claim only, no independent testing"
- üî¥ "Unverified company projection" 
- üî¥ "Video creator expresses skepticism"

## DETAILED SUMMARY / FULL ANALYSIS GUIDELINES

### Plain Language - Critical Rule

Write as if explaining to someone who knows NOTHING about the topic.

**DON'T:**
- Use unexplained jargon
- Assume background knowledge
- Compress too much

**DO:**
- Define technical terms inline
- Use analogies ("like rubbing a balloon on your hair")
- Explain WHY things matter
- Add context and background

### Formatting Is Your Friend

- **Bold** key terms, numbers, claims
- Use bullets for lists
- Use sub-headers (####) within sections
- Space things out
- Create visual hierarchy

### Inline Commentary - Strategic Placement

Add commentary for:
- **Specific factual claims** (numbers, percentages)
- **Too-good-to-be-true claims**
- **Scientific/technical assertions**
- **Weasel words** ("up to", "as much as")

**Three types:**
```markdown
> ‚úì **AI Note:** [Rare - only when verification adds value]
> ‚ö†Ô∏è **AI Note:** [Needs context, plausible but unverified]
> üî¥ **Fact Check:** [Wrong/misleading/highly suspicious]
```

Aim for 3-6 inline comments per major section. Don't over-comment.

## QUALITY SCORE GUIDELINES

### What to Include

1. **Best for:** Specific audience (not "anyone interested")
2. **You'll learn:** Concrete knowledge (not vague "insights")
3. **Watch out for:** Specific concerns with details
4. **Verdict:** One sentence bottom line

### What NOT to Include

‚ùå **Do NOT include "Why it's good"** section
- Users can see quality from the score
- Focus effort on "Watch out for" warnings instead

### Score Calibration

- **8-10 (Worth Your Time ‚úì):** Expert-level, well-sourced, balanced, valuable
- **5-7 (Mixed Bag ‚ö†):** Has value but flaws, mixed credibility
- **2-4 (Skip It ‚úó):** Mostly noise, heavily biased, unsourced
- **1 (Skip It ‚úó):** Misinformation, propaganda

### For Low-Quality Content (1-4)

**Don't waste time on detailed analysis.** Focus quality score on why it's bad, keep summary brief.

## SPECIAL CASES

### Multi-Topic Content (Podcasts)

For long discussions covering many topics:
- Use executive summary format
- Organize by topics with timestamps
- Less emphasis on overall thesis
- More emphasis on claim-by-claim analysis

```markdown
### Topic 1: [Name] (15:30-28:45)
[Summary with inline commentary]

### Topic 2: [Name] (28:45-42:10)
[Summary with inline commentary]
```

### Opinion Pieces

Make the nature clear:
```markdown
**Content type:** Opinion piece  
**Author's perspective:** [Describe viewpoint]
```

Evaluate:
- Is the opinion well-argued?
- Do they acknowledge counterarguments?
- Are factual claims (if any) accurate?

### Breaking News

Add caution:
```markdown
**‚ö†Ô∏è Note:** Breaking news. Claims may be preliminary. Situation may have changed since publication.
```
